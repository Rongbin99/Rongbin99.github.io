## Older Than You Think

For context, the oldest bus still operational in the [TTC's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toronto_Transit_Commission) active bus fleet are the [2007 Orion VII NGs (Next Gens) (Hybrid)](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/Toronto_Transit_Commission_1200-1423#Details). The [New Flyer Industries (NFI) D40i](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/Ottawa-Carleton_Regional_Transit_Commission_4203-4273#Details) in OC Transpo's bus fleet are from 2004. That makes these NFI D40i's older than me and 3 years older then the Orions, of which OC Transpo [retired](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/ottawa-oc-transpo-bus-sale-1.5331534) them [all in 2020](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/Ottawa-Carleton_Regional_Transit_Commission#Retired_bus_roster).

Ottawa, the Capital of Canada, is a heavily car-oriented city with subpar transit service and ridiciously high fares. As Ottawa and OC Transpo undergo massive transit upgrades with the construction and extentions of their LRTs by repurposing their iconic Transitway, a history of Ottawa's early transit network is slowly being erased.

The [Transitway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transitway_(Ottawa)) was a [Bus Rapid Transit (BRT)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit) network that consisted of bus only right of ways. This essentially established a highway network exclusive to OC Transpo buses with stations, known as Transitway stops, for the servicing of commuters. With the first segment opening in 1983, a fraction of its prime still remains today.

OC Transpo operates a significant fleet of New Flyer D40i (Invero) buses built by [New Flyer Industries (NFI)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Flyer) between 2003 and 2006. Due to poor demand, the Invero lineup was discountinued in 2007 with OC Transpo being the main operator of them. Having a distinct hump at the front that resembles a beluga, these buses introduced a top mounted HVAC system, which improved airflow circulation, that later made its way (wordy so fix later) to New Flyer's next line of buses, the [Xcelsior](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Flyer_Xcelsior) ([XDE40](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/New_Flyer_Industries_XDE40), [XDE60](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/New_Flyer_Industries_XDE60), [XE40](https://cptdb.ca/wiki/index.php/New_Flyer_Industries_XE40), etc.).

Out of 326 buses ordered and operational, only 29 are still actively in service as of April 2026, according to CPTDB (9 according to OC Transpo's [website](https://web.archive.org/web/20260417203925/https://www.octranspo.com/en/our-services/vehicles/), down from [55 from Jan 2026](https://web.archive.org/web/20260118143529/https://www.octranspo.com/en/our-services/vehicles/)). That's 8.9% (2.8%) of the original fleet that are still driving the roads of Ottawa, and will certainly be retired very soon marking the end of the Belugas. 13 units were retired alone since Jan 2026 (46 according to OC Transpo). The D40i's are going extinct, so definitely enjoy them while you still can!
