## The Eglinton LRT

The Eglinton LRT (Light Rail Transit) is a much anticipated line that runs across Toronto from [Kennedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kennedy_station) to [Mount Dennis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Dennis_station) through Midtown. By providing another East / West transit line for the city, it provides some much needed redundancy and relief for the congested [Line 2: Bloor-Danforth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Line_2_Bloor%E2%80%93Danforth).

The Light Rail Vehicles (LRVs) used on Line 5 are identical to the ones used on the [GRT ION](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion_rapid_transit) in [Kitchener/Waterloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_Municipality_of_Waterloo). The only difference is the GRT ION has operator cabs on both ends allowing them to switch directions, whereas the ones on Eglinton only have one cab thus they chain them opposite to each other.

## The Wreckage Era

Construction of the Eglinton LRT date back to 2011 with [tunneling beginning in 2013](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/machines-begin-tunnelling-for-eglinton-crosstown-lrt-1.1368317). During this time, Mayor [Rob Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Ford) infamously pushed for the [construction of more subways](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/mayor-rob-ford-loses-toronto-subway-vote-1.1213192) instead of "glorified" streetcar LRTs as they occupy valuable road space and are slow. Looking back at it now, Ford might've been right.

With construction underway, this era of wreckage would last for almost an entire decade. Throughout the years, numerous businesses along the Eglinton corridor have [closed due to lower foot traffic](https://www.ctvnews.ca/toronto/article/eglinton-lrt-construction-has-forced-140-business-closures-city-councillors-say/), and non-stop [patch updates](https://transittoronto.ca/archives/weblog/2014/12/03-ttc_revisi.shtml) to the Kennedy Station bus platforms caused confusion and inconvenience to commuters. Eglinton Ave underwent some major road widening to accomodate the new dedicated right of way for the LRT, and the [identity of Eglinton](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/how-toronto-main-street-is-coping-with-lrt-construction-1.2827232) as a whole was never the same.
